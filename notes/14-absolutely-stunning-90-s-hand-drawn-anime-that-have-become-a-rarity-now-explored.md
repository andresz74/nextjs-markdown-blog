# 14 Absolutely Stunning 90's Hand-Drawn Anime That Have Become A Rarity Now - Explored
## One Sentence Summary:
This video highlights 14 iconic '90s handdrawn anime that revolutionized animation, storytelling, and cultural impact.

### Main Points:
1. Ghost in the Shell combined traditional animation with early CGI, influencing cyberpunk aesthetics and philosophy.
2. Princess Monoke showcased breathtaking handpainted backgrounds emphasizing environmental themes.
3. Cowboy Bebop set a stylish, jazzy tone blending genres, becoming a standard for cool, mature anime.
4. Neon Genesis Evangelion challenged mecha conventions with psychological depth and raw, intense animation.
5. Ninja Scroll delivered brutal, stylized action that became a blueprint for mature, dark fantasy anime.
6. Ginro the Wolf Brigade used realistic, weighty animation to explore dystopian themes and moral ambiguity.
7. The Vision of Escaflowne fused mecha and fantasy with expressive, textured animation and emotional storytelling.
8. Patlabor 2 proved mecha films can explore political themes with cinematic realism and tension.
9. Perfect Blue blurred reality and hallucination, setting a new standard for psychological thrillers in anime.
10. Revolutionary Girl Utena redefined the magical girl genre with surreal symbolism and bold visual metaphors.
11. Macros Plus combined high-speed aerial combat with AI themes, foreshadowing modern virtual entertainment.
12. His and Her Circumstances used experimental animation to explore teenage emotions and societal masks.
13. Slayers mixed humor, magic, and adventure with over-the-top spell effects and lively character designs.
14. Marmalade Boy epitomized '90s shojo romance with expressive visuals, fashion, and complicated love dynamics.

### Takeaways:
1. Handdrawn animation in the '90s pushed artistic boundaries and set standards for visual storytelling.
2. Iconic series often combine strong visuals with deep philosophical or emotional themes.
3. Genre-bending and experimentation in animation continue to influence modern anime creators.
4. Many of these classics blend traditional techniques with early digital elements, enriching their aesthetic.
5. Timeless anime balance stunning visuals, memorable characters, and relevant themes that resonate decades later.
